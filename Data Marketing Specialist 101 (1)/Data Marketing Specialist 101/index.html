When you market a product or service, the goal is to achieve specific effects out of your audience. These goals can be frameworked into five categories that all start with the letter A, hence the 5 As. The 5 As are as follows:

Aware:

You want your audience to see your advertising both online and offline.

Appeal:

You want your audience to be interested about your product or service

Ask:

You want your audience to be curious and ask about your product or service or look for a review in order for them to learn more about it.

Act:

You want your customers to make the decision to buy your product or service.

Advocate:

You want your customers to enjoy your product or service, so that they leave a positive review on social media or advocate their experience to their friends and family.

There is no single pathway to becoming a digital marketing specialist.

All industries require digital marketing, but you still have to consider the different channels you can use for specific industries.

Art:

Basic design understanding will help digital marketing specialists decide if the design aligns with the branding or not.


Writing:

Writing class promotes creative exploration and writing skills that are crucial for effective marketing. Having an established base of good vocabulary and context is essential when writing advertisements.

Time Management:

It is important to know how to manage a schedule during a marketing campaign. This will help you create an efficient timeline that works for a digital marketing campaign.


Analytical Skills:

The ability to analyze data lets you become aware of moving trends and let you adapt to the mistakes of the previous campaign.


Multitasking:

Digital marketting is often not a one time project. Many campaigns and projects might be going on at the same time. It is important for you to understand which tasks to prioritize and which tasks you can do simultaneously.
